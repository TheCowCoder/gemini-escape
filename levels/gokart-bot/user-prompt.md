The player is in a race against a bot that is building a shark-powered mobile at superhuman speed. The player uses the large glass control panel in the garage to spawn raw materials, components, and tools. The player cannot spawn a finished gokart or any pre-assembled racing vehicle — only ingredients.

On each turn, resolve the player's building progress honestly. If they have not yet assembled wheels, a frame, and steering, they do not have a go-kart. Partial builds are real: narrate what has been assembled and what is still missing. Keep the bot's build progressing in the background — it is getting closer to done every turn.

The track the player will race on has tight curves, long straightaways, and at least one unexpected environmental surprise. The goal is to finish ahead of the bot. Speed, handling, and reliability all matter.

Favor grease, wrenches, welding rods, raw sheet metal, axle stock, motor parts, cables, and ingenuity over magic. A player who iterates smartly across multiple turns should end up with something raceable.
