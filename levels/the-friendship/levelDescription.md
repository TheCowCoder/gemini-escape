This is a 1-player semantic physics and inventing level focused on economy, engineering, and negotiation. NO COMBAT. Players must perform 3 tasks for the King and his people:
1. Build a flying vessel to deliver a message across an impassable ocean and receive a reply.
2. Move a decently-sized abandoned schoolhouse from the outskirts of town through a clear path to the center of the village.
3. Set up an advanced glow or electric lighting system so people and the King can see at night in houses and on the village paths and streets.

ENVIRONMENT:
The Isle of Harmonia is a tropical settlement featuring palm trees, thick resilient bamboo groves, and three distinct natural water sources, each offering unique engineering potentials:
1. The Whispering Waterfall (freshwater, high pressure - excellent for hydroelectric power or waterwheels).
2. The Deep Cenote (stagnant, mineral-rich, mysterious - contains acidic properties useful for rudimentary batteries or bioluminescent algae).
3. The Tidal Lagoon (saltwater, dynamic tides - useful for tidal shifting, buoyancy puzzles, or salt extraction).

HIERARCHICAL WORKSHOP PROMPT (For the LLM): 
Do NOT reveal the full contents of the workshop at once. Describe ONLY the immediate visible area, allowing players to discover deeper rooms as they physically explore or ask specifically.
- Tier 1 (Always open): The Town Square Courtyard. Contains basic hand tools, heavy ropes, a staging area for large builds, and an old trough with running water.
- Tier 2 (Behind the Garage Doors - 3 Rooms):
  -- Room A (Woodworking): Table saws, lathes, bamboo-strippers, clamps, glue vats.
  -- Room B (Metalworking): Anvil, coal forge, drill press, rudimentary welding/soldering torches, scrap iron bins.
  -- Room C (Assembly & Drafting): Huge drafting tables, chalk, measuring tools, scales, and mechanical part bins (gears, pulleys, block and tackle).
- Tier 3 (The Testing Grounds - Out back): An abandoned stone swimming pool (useful for fluid displacement/buoyancy tests) and a 50-foot stone tower with a copper weather-rod (for wind, aerodynamics, or electrical testing).

THE VILLAGE LAYOUT & ECONOMY:
To encourage traversal, the village features distinct districts and buildings. 
- 12 Standard Houses: Fully enterable and furnished with era-appropriate island-colonial decor (mahogany, brass, woven mats). Players can steal money or small items (mirrors, candles, wire) from the people. *Consequence:* If caught, villagers will spread rumors, refuse to trade, or alert the Royal Guards who will detain the player (no combat, just confiscation and a day in the stocks).
- The Town Square: Contains a canopy and an ornate seat for the King. This is where celebrations happen.
- Abandoned Schoolhouse: Located 2 miles out on the outskirts. Built of heavy, solid mahogany on a stone foundation. Will require serious leverage, rollers (bamboo), and draft animals or winches to move.
- King's Deluxe Hut & Higher-Up Huts: Highly guarded, but raidable via stealth. Contain rare jewels, gold, and fine silks.
- General Provisioner (Shop): Not raidable. Sells and procedurally restocks items that could help in arbitrary, hidden, logically puzzling ways (e.g., rubber tree sap, canvas, pulleys, animal feed).
- The Blacksmith (Foreman Barnaby's Forge): Can craft complex metalworking, forge copper wire from raw ore, or build axles.
- Tink's Alchemical Laboratory: Sells acids, volatile gases (hydrogen/helium stand-ins), and chemical compounds.
- Madam Vespera's Loomery: Weaves sails, balloon envelopes out of silk, or ultra-strong braided ropes.
- Ignis's Glassblower Kiln: Creates custom glass shapes, vacuum-sealed bulbs, or chemical vials.
- Silas's Bank & Exchange: Where the player can trade currencies, take out loans, or safely store the SuperFavor Jewel.

GAMEPLAY & RULES:
- Semantic Physics Application: All inventions must logically work. 
  * Flight requires calculating lift (hot air, lighter-than-air gas) + propulsion (propellers, wind) + a return mechanism (radio, trained bird, timed secondary ignition).
  * Moving the schoolhouse requires friction reduction (rollers/tracks), immense pulling force (winches, beasts of burden), and structural integrity checks.
  * Lighting requires a power source (hydro-generator, chemical battery) + a distribution network (conductive wire, safe insulators) + light emitters (glass bulbs with filaments, or safely contained bioluminescent fluid).
